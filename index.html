I. Executive Overview

This document outlines a comprehensive deployment strategy tailored for Datastream Digital Sdn Bhd (DST), expanding upon previous high-level proposals and strategic timelines. It directly addresses DST's request for a detailed delivery plan, demonstrating robust execution capabilities and providing a clear roadmap for the phased implementation of critical cloud services. This plan is intended to instill confidence and provide the necessary clarity for DST to proceed with its transformative Cloud Zone initiative. [1]

DST's strategic ambition is to become a leading regional cloud service provider, underscored by objectives to achieve $1 billion in annual revenue and transform Brunei's digital landscape. [1] The Cloud Zone initiative, with a projected Net Present Value (NPV) exceeding $1 billion and an Internal Rate of Return (IRR) of 68%, is central to this transformation. [1]

The deployment strategy encompasses a phased journey, commencing with the establishment of foundational services on DST's existing Unified National Network (UNN) infrastructure. This initial phase will be followed by a strategic and carefully managed transition to white-labeled offshore Cloud Service Provider (CSP) environments, specifically leveraging Amazon Web Services (AWS), Google Cloud, and Microsoft Azure. [1] This multi-stage, multi-platform approach is designed to optimize capabilities, manage costs effectively, and provide DST with the flexibility to utilize best-of-breed services from leading CSPs, all managed and delivered seamlessly. [1]

The core services to be deployed include Infrastructure as a Service (IaaS), comprehensive Migration Services, scalable Data Storage solutions, and resilient Disaster Recovery as a Service (DRaaS). [1] These services will be rolled out in a phased manner, designed to deliver incrementally increasing value. A foundational Cloud Landing Zone (LZ) is paramount, providing the secure, governed, and scalable environment necessary for all subsequent service deployments. [1] Once the LZ is established, certain deployment activities for the use cases can proceed in parallel, accelerating time-to-market. This approach is designed to unlock significant market opportunities, including a $13.7 billion addressable market for the four key services in ASEAN. [1]

The successful execution of this multi-faceted deployment extends beyond technical implementation. It necessitates a concurrent evolution of DST's operational model to effectively manage a cloud brokerage and managed services business. [1] This operational transformation is a critical underpinning for realizing the projected revenue and market leadership.

II. Introduction and Strategic Context

Purpose of this Document

The purpose of this document is to provide DST with a detailed, actionable deployment approach for its Cloud Zone strategy. It outlines the sequential and parallel activities required to successfully launch the four core cloud service offerings: IaaS, Migration Services, Data Storage, and DRaaS. This plan builds upon the strategic groundwork, market analysis, and business case development undertaken during the initial 14-week joint engagement between DST and Ollion. [1]

DST's Cloud Ambition

DST has articulated a clear and ambitious vision: to transform from its current market position into Brunei's leading cloud-agnostic broker and a significant regional cloud services player. [1] The establishment of the Cloud Zone is the cornerstone of this strategy, pivotal for achieving the stated financial objective of $1 billion in annual revenue as a digital service provider and realizing a projected 68% IRR from cloud operations. [1] This initiative directly supports DST's 2024 objectives to establish leadership in the local cloud market and leverage cloud technology for national transformation and growth. [1]

Service Portfolio Overview

The Cloud Zone will initially focus on four key service offerings, as defined in the "2025 Cloud Zone Strategy" proposal [1]:

  • Infrastructure as a Service (IaaS): Evolving from leveraging existing data center usage to a comprehensive cloud brokerage model, offering choice across AWS, Google Cloud, and Microsoft Azure, orchestrated by DST. [1]
  • Migration & Managed Services: Facilitating secure and efficient migration to cloud environments and providing ongoing management, leveraging the DST-Ollion alliance for deep cloud expertise. [1]
  • Data Storage: Offering tiered storage solutions, including high-speed critical data storage and cost-efficient standard storage, initially leveraging DST's existing infrastructure and regional submarine cable links. [1]
  • Disaster Recovery as a Service (DRaaS): Providing highly reliable DRaaS by utilizing Brunei's strategic submarine cable connections and existing data center footprint, positioning DST as a regional hub for business continuity. [1]

Core Principles of Deployment

The deployment of the Cloud Zone will adhere to the following core principles [1]:

  • Phased Rollout: Services will be introduced incrementally, allowing for managed investment, risk mitigation, and iterative value delivery. The initial focus will be on UNN-based services.
  • Use Case Driven: The deployment is structured around the four key use cases, each building on the capabilities of the previous ones and delivering progressively more sophisticated value to the market.
  • Landing Zone First: A robust, secure, and well-governed Cloud Landing Zone is a non-negotiable prerequisite for the deployment of any service.
  • Parallel Execution: Following the establishment of the LZ, certain development and deployment workstreams for different use cases can be executed in parallel to optimize timelines.
  • White-Labeling Strategy: Hyperscaler services from AWS, Google Cloud, and Azure will be offered as DST-branded solutions, with DST managing the customer relationship, billing, and first-line support, providing a seamless experience. [1]

"Crawl, Walk, Run" Approach

The "Crawl, Walk, Run" approach, outlined in the initial revenue projections [1], is a strategic imperative that guides this deployment plan. The "Crawl" phase (Year 0-1), emphasizing the development of minimum viable capacity for IaaS and Migration services on UNN, is critical. This allows DST to build essential operational experience, test market reception, and establish credibility with lower initial capital expenditure, particularly by monetizing existing infrastructure as planned for IaaS and Data Storage. [1] This approach de-risks the substantial long-term investment by ensuring foundational capabilities and market understanding are in place before scaling aggressively with multiple hyperscaler partnerships in the "Walk" (Year 1-2) and "Run" (Year 3 onwards) phases. [1]

Strategic Imperatives for DST

While the specific business drivers for DST's initiative are proprietary, this deployment approach is architected to align with common strategic imperatives such as enhanced agility, operational efficiency, innovation enablement, cost optimization, and robust business resilience. The proposed phased deployment, centered around four interdependent use cases, is designed to deliver incremental and measurable value, ensuring that each stage of the transformation directly contributes to DST's overarching business objectives. [1]

III. Foundational Landing Zone Deployment

A. Landing Zone: The Cornerstone of Cloud Adoption

The Cloud Landing Zone (LZ) is the cornerstone of DST's Cloud Zone strategy. Its purpose is to establish a secure, compliant, scalable, and well-governed foundation that will host and manage all cloud services offered by DST. This applies whether services are delivered from DST's own UNN infrastructure or through white-labeled offerings from AWS, Google Cloud, and Azure. The LZ is the non-negotiable first step and a prerequisite for the successful deployment of all subsequent use cases. [1] A landing zone is a pre-configured environment designed according to best practices to support enterprise-scale cloud adoption, enforcing security, ensuring compliance, driving operational efficiency, and accelerating workload deployment. [1]

Key Objectives for the LZ include: [1]

  • Standardize environments for rapid service deployment.
  • Enforce security, compliance, and governance consistently.
  • Enable efficient, centralized operations and monitoring.
  • Clearly demarcate responsibilities (UNN, CSPs, customer).
  • Facilitate integrated multi-cloud cost management and billing.

B. Initial UNN-Based Landing Zone Architecture (Private Cloud Focus)

The initial deployment phase will establish a robust landing zone within DST's existing UNN infrastructure. This private cloud landing zone will support initial use cases and pave the way for CSP integration. [1] The LZ will comprise several core components, initially focused on UNN-based services, while architected with CSP-agnostic principles for future hyperscaler integration. [1]

Core Infrastructure (IaaS on UNN):

  • Compute: Leverage DST's existing virtualization platform (e.g., VMware) for VMs. Define resource provisioning and capacity management. [1]
  • Network: Establish a segmented network architecture within UNN for secure isolation and controlled traffic flow (e.g., logical segments, internal firewalls, hub-and-spoke model). Strictly control external connectivity. [1]
  • Storage: Utilize on-premise UNN storage solutions. Implement capacity planning, data management policies (tiering, backup), and performance optimization. [1]

Identity and Access Management (IAM):

  • Initial Focus (UNN): Implement a centralized IAM solution for UNN-based services, defining roles, policies, and access controls. [1]
  • CSP Agnostic Principle: Design with federation capabilities to integrate seamlessly with AWS IAM, Google Cloud IAM, and Azure Active Directory. [1] Integrate with existing DST identity systems where appropriate. [1] Enforce Role-Based Access Control (RBAC). [1]

Security and Governance Controls:

  • Initial Focus (UNN): Establish security baselines, implement core security controls (firewalls, IDS/IPS, WAFs), and deploy security monitoring tools for the UNN environment. Address local data sovereignty requirements. [1]
  • CSP Agnostic Principle: Develop a security and compliance framework (e.g., ISO 27001, SOC 2) applicable across UNN and white-labeled CSP services. Implement tools for a unified security posture view. [1] Establish comprehensive security policies and compliance measures. [1] Implement centralized logging, monitoring, and auditing. [1]

Operations Management:

  • Initial Focus (UNN): Implement solutions for centralized logging, monitoring, alerting, patching, and backup/recovery for UNN-hosted services. [1]
  • CSP Agnostic Principle: Select or design operational tools that integrate with CSP-native services (e.g., CloudWatch, Azure Monitor, Google Cloud's operations suite) for a single pane of glass. [1] Define provisioning/de-provisioning processes and ITIL-aligned change/configuration management. [1]

Orchestration and Automation:

  • Initial Focus (UNN): Implement orchestration and automation tools for provisioning, configuring, and managing UNN resources. [1]
  • CSP Agnostic Principle: Design a service catalog and orchestration layer extensible to multiple CSPs, potentially using a Cloud Management Platform (CMP) or Infrastructure-as-Code (IaC). [1]

Cost Management & Billing Foundation:

  • Initial Focus (UNN): Establish mechanisms for metering and tracking UNN resource consumption. [1]
  • CSP Agnostic Principle: Design a framework for ingesting CSP billing data, aggregating costs, and enabling integrated billing for customers. [1]

The design philosophy for the LZ must be inherently "CSP-agnostic" from the outset to reduce future integration friction and enable agile cloud brokerage. [1]

C. Transition Pathway to White-Labeled CSP Landing Zones (AWS, Azure, Google Cloud)

Following UNN LZ utilization, a strategic transition to Ollion's white-labeled CSP landing zones will unlock enhanced capabilities. [1]

  • Rationale for Transition: Leverage CSP-specific strengths (e.g., AWS for broad IaaS, Azure for Microsoft workloads, Google Cloud for data analytics/ML), enhanced scalability, access to advanced services, global reach, and cost optimization via pay-as-you-go models. [1]
  • CSP Landing Zone Design Principles: Adhere to Well-Architected Frameworks (AWS, Azure), implement multi-account/subscription strategies for isolation and governance, distinguish between Platform Landing Zones (PLZs for shared services managed by Ollion) and Application Landing Zones (ALZs for DST workloads), and utilize Infrastructure as Code (IaC) for consistent deployments (e.g., CloudFormation, ARM/Bicep, Terraform). [1]
  • Specific CSP Landing Zone Components: Leverage native CSP services like AWS Control Tower, AWS Organizations, VPC, Security Hub; Azure Management Groups, Azure Policy, VNets, Microsoft Defender for Cloud; Google Cloud Folders/Projects, VPC networks, IAM, Security Command Center. Ollion's white-labeling will abstract these specifics, providing a unified interface. [1]

Deployment Activities (LZ) [1]

  1. Detailed Design Workshops (architecture, network, security, IAM, operations, automation).
  2. Infrastructure Provisioning (UNN compute, storage, network for LZ management plane).
  3. Tool Selection and Implementation (IAM, security, monitoring, orchestration, cost management).
  4. Policy Definition and Configuration (codify security, governance, operational policies).
  5. Integration and Automation (initial scripts for common tasks).
  6. Testing and Validation (all LZ components, security, operations, automation).
  7. Documentation and Training (LZ architecture, operations, policies; train DST teams).

IV. Phased Deployment of Use Cases

The deployment of DST's Cloud Zone services will occur in phases, building upon the last and delivering increasing value. This aligns with the "Crawl, Walk, Run" strategy. [1]

A. Overall Phasing Strategy [1]

  • Phase 1 (UNN Foundation & Core Enablement): Leverage UNN infrastructure to monetize assets, address data sovereignty, and build initial cloud operational capabilities. Key services: UNN-based Data Storage and basic IaaS.
  • Phase 2 (Cloud Adoption & Brokerage Initiation): Introduce services facilitating cloud adoption and begin cloud brokerage. Includes Migration Services, foundational DRaaS on UNN, and preparing for/initiating first white-labeled hyperscaler integration.
  • Phase 3 (Full Multi-Cloud Brokerage & Advanced Services): Achieve comprehensive multi-cloud brokerage (AWS, Google Cloud, Azure). Offer advanced DRaaS (UNN & CSPs) and expand with sophisticated managed services.

V. Multi-Cloud Integration and White-Labeled Service Delivery

A. CSP Selection Rationale per Use Case/Service [1]

Selection of AWS, Azure, and Google Cloud for particular use cases will align CSP strengths with DST's requirements. Ollion's white-labeling provides a unified experience. Examples:

  • Google Cloud: Favored for Use Case 2 (Data Storage/Analytics) if DST has significant big data/advanced analytics needs (BigQuery, Vertex AI).
  • Microsoft Azure: Preferred for modernizing/hosting .NET applications or Microsoft ecosystem workloads (Use Case 3) (Azure App Service, AKS, Azure SQL).
  • Amazon Web Services (AWS): Primary for general-purpose IaaS, broad application hosting (Use Cases 1 & 3), or specific DRaaS due to mature services.

Ollion will provide justification, considering existing investments, features, performance, cost, and data sovereignty.

B. Ollion's White-Labeling Operating Model for DST [1]

Ollion delivers a fully managed, white-labeled multi-cloud experience to simplify complexity, provide consistent governance, and optimize performance/cost.

  • Service Abstraction and Presentation: Unified Ollion interface (service portal, standardized service catalog abstracting CSP specifics), Ollion branding. [1]
  • Billing Management and Cost Optimization: Consolidated Ollion invoice, cost allocation/showback, proactive cost optimization (monitoring, waste elimination, rightsizing, leveraging CSP pricing models). [1]
  • Support and Escalation Procedures: Ollion as single point of contact (Level 1/2 support), defined SLAs, managed escalation to CSPs (Level 3), white-label support experience. [1]
  • Service Management and Governance: ITIL-aligned processes, policy enforcement, change management, performance/compliance reporting. Ollion manages complexities of offshore CSPs (data sovereignty, support models, vendor management). [1]

C. Cross-Cloud Governance, Security, and Compliance Framework [1]

Ollion will establish a unified framework for consistent governance, security, and compliance across UNN and multi-CSP environments.

  • Unified Governance Framework: Policies, standards, processes for resource organization, tagging, cost management, provisioning, access control.
  • Centralized or Federated Identity Management: Consistent user access/control (federating DST's IdP or centralized solution for SSO).
  • Consistent Security Posture Management: CSPM tools, security baselines enforced via native CSP tools (Security Hub, Azure Policy, Google Security Command Center) and third-party solutions, managed by Ollion.
  • Compliance Mapping and Reporting: Map DST's regulatory requirements to UNN/CSP controls, implement configurations, provide reporting for audits.
  • Automation: Leverage IaC for policy enforcement, automated security checks, and compliance reporting.

D. Network Integration and Interoperability [1]

Ollion will design and manage a comprehensive network integration strategy.

  • Connectivity between UNN and CSP Landing Zones: Secure, reliable connectivity (Site-to-Site VPNs or Dedicated Interconnects like AWS Direct Connect, Azure ExpressRoute, Google Cloud Interconnect).
  • Inter-CSP Connectivity: Solutions for direct, private communication between workloads on different CSPs if needed (CSP peering, third-party NaaS).
  • DNS Strategy: Unified DNS for consistent name resolution (integrating UNN DNS with CSP DNS or centralized solution).
  • IP Addressing and Routing Plans: Comprehensive IPAM to avoid conflicts and ensure efficient routing.
  • Network Security: Consistent controls (firewalls, NSGs/Security Groups, WAFs) managed by Ollion.
  • Considerations: Optimize for data transfer costs and latency.

E. Other Cross-Cutting Considerations [1]

  • Skills Development and Organizational Readiness (DST Internal): Continuous skills development for technical, sales, and support teams. Potential organizational restructuring for cloud brokerage operations.
  • Partner Ecosystem Management: Dedicated DST resources for managing relationships with AWS, Google Cloud, Azure; joint GTM strategies.
  • Customer Onboarding and Support: Streamlined onboarding processes; tiered support model with DST owning the primary relationship.
  • Branding and Customer Experience: Holistic approach to branding, marketing, and communication to establish DST as the primary provider. Consistent DST-branded experience across all touchpoints. [1]

VI. Detailed Migration Approach (UNN to White-Labeled CSPs) [1]

A structured methodology to minimize risk and downtime.

A. Assessment and Discovery of UNN Workloads

  • Comprehensive inventory (applications, data, infrastructure), dependency mapping, performance baselining, security/compliance assessment. Tools: Azure Migrate, AWS Application Discovery Service.
  • Deliverable: Cloud Readiness Assessment Report.

B. Migration Planning

  • Workload analysis and prioritization, migration strategy per workload (The "6 Rs": Rehost, Replatform, Refactor, Repurchase, Retain, Retire), wave planning, tool selection (native CSP tools like AWS MGN, Azure Migrate, Google Cloud Migrate; third-party tools).
  • Deliverable: Detailed Migration Plan.

C. Execution Strategies

  • Target environment provisioning on CSPs (using IaC), application migration (per "R" strategy), data migration and synchronization (initial bulk transfer, ongoing sync, integrity validation).
  • Deliverable: Migrated Workloads Operational on CSPs.

D. Testing and Validation

  • Functional testing, performance testing, security testing, User Acceptance Testing (UAT) by DST, DR testing.
  • Deliverables: Test Plans, Test Reports, UAT Sign-off.

E. Cutover and Go-Live

  • Detailed cutover plan (pre-checks, activities, rollback, communication), final data sync, DNS changes, go/no-go checkpoints, post-go-live hypercare.
  • Deliverable: Go-Live Confirmation, Post-Migration Support Plan.

F. Decommissioning of UNN Resources

  • Phased decommissioning of UNN servers/storage/network, data archival/cleanup, license management.
  • Deliverable: UNN Decommissioning Report.

VIII. Governance and Project Management [1]

A. Project Governance Structure

  • Steering Committee (Ollion & DST Execs): Strategic oversight, key decisions, issue resolution.
  • Project Management Team (Ollion PM, DST PM): Day-to-day execution, progress tracking, risk/issue management.
  • Technical Workstream Leads (Ollion): Technical delivery, coordination with DST SMEs.
  • Communication Plan: Formal plan for status reports, risk updates, milestone reviews.
  • RACI Matrix: To clarify roles for key phases/deliverables (Responsible, Accountable, Consulted, Informed).

B. Risk Management Framework

  • Continuous process: Risk identification, assessment (likelihood/impact), mitigation strategies, contingency plans, Risk Register maintained and reviewed.
  • Deliverable: Risk Register and Mitigation Plan.

C. Change Management Process

  • Formal process: Change request submission, impact assessment (scope, schedule, cost, resources, risk), approval by appropriate authority, implementation and communication.

D. Quality Assurance Plan

  • Define quality standards/acceptance criteria, formal review/approval cycles for deliverables, comprehensive testing strategy, lessons-learned sessions.

E. Operational Readiness Review (ORR) Plan

  • Conducted before major service/use case go-live. ORR checklists (infrastructure, monitoring, backup/DR, security, procedures, training, support handover). Key input to go/no-go decision. AWS ORR custom lens adaptable.

IX. Key Considerations and Recommendations [1]

A. Technical Debt Management

  • Identification/assessment during migration. Strategic remediation (during refactoring, prioritized, or accepted short-term with plan). Prevent new debt via cloud-native principles, IaC, code reviews.
  • Recommendation: DST allocate resources for ongoing technical debt management.

B. Skills and Training

  • Skills assessment of DST teams. Tailored training plan (Ollion portal, cloud best practices, specific tech, financial management). Encourage continuous learning.
  • Recommendation: DST invest in upskilling for maximum benefit and collaboration. [1]

C. Vendor Management (Ollion's Role)

  • Ollion as single point of contact for DST. Ollion manages CSP relationships (technical/commercial, support, updates). Ollion monitors CSP performance/SLAs.
  • Recommendation: DST establish clear communication/review processes with Ollion.

D. Security and Compliance Posture (Ongoing)

  • Continuous security monitoring by Ollion. Joint incident response plan. Ongoing compliance management support from Ollion (audits, evidence). Ollion manages security updates/patching for IaaS/platform.
  • Recommendation: DST actively participate in security/compliance reviews with Ollion.

E. Future Scalability and Evolution

  • Scalable architecture design. Ollion to evolve service catalog with DST. Ollion to advise on new tech adoption (AI/ML, serverless, edge).
  • Recommendation: DST establish regular strategic review cycle with Ollion.

X. Conclusion: Accelerating DST's Transformation

This comprehensive deployment approach provides a structured and actionable pathway for DST to systematically build its Cloud Zone and realize its ambitious strategic objectives: to become Brunei's leading cloud-agnostic broker, a significant regional cloud services player, and to achieve its targeted financial growth of $1 billion in annual revenue with a 68% IRR. [1]

The phased rollout, centered around four key use casesIaaS, Migration Services, Data Storage, and DRaaSis designed to deliver incrementally increasing value, manage risk, and build capabilities progressively, following the "Crawl, Walk, Run" methodology. [1] The journey begins with the critical establishment of a robust Cloud Landing Zone, followed by the monetization of existing UNN assets through sovereign cloud offerings. It then expands strategically to incorporate white-labeled services from global hyperscalersAWS, Google Cloud, and Azurepositioning DST as a comprehensive intermediary capable of offering choice, flexibility, and integrated management. [1] The culmination of this deployment will see DST offering advanced, high-value services such as sophisticated hybrid DRaaS and comprehensive multi-cloud Managed Services, solidifying its leadership in the region. [1]

The successful execution of this plan, supported by robust governance, detailed migration strategies, and a proactive approach to key considerations like skills development and security, will not only unlock significant revenue potential but will also fundamentally transform DST's role in the digital ecosystem. [1] It requires a concerted effort across technology deployment, operational model evolution, skills development, and strategic partner management.

Ollion remains committed to partnering with DST throughout this transformative journey, providing the necessary expertise in cloud strategy, design, implementation, and operationalization to ensure the Cloud Zone becomes a resounding success. [1] The immediate call to action is to proceed with the detailed project planning, resource allocation, and initiation of Phase 0: the design and build of the foundational Cloud Landing Zone, which will serve as the bedrock for all future Cloud Zone services and DST's accelerated transformation. [1]
